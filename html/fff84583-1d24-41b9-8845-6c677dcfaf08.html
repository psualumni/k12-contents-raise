Activity (15 minutes)

Up to this point in the unit, students have written and interpreted equations representing exponential change that were meaningful for non-negative input values. In this lesson, they interpret the meaning of a negative value in a context. The independent variable is time, \(t\), so negative values of \(t\) refer to times before \(t\) is 0.

For the last question, to find the time when the coral had the given volume, students may:

  • Calculate the values of \(y\) when \(t\) is –3, –4, –5, etc. (i.e., extend the table of values, if they previously created one) until they reach 37.5 or a value close to it.
  • Repeatedly divide by 2 (or multiply by \(\frac12\)) the value of \(y\) when \(t\) is –2 until it approaches or hits 37.5.
  • Use the equation \(1,200\cdot 2^t=37.5\) to find the value of \(2^t\) and then reason about \(t\) from there.

Look for students using these or other strategies so they can share later.

Making graphing technology available gives students an opportunity to choose appropriate tools strategically.

Launch

Arrange students in groups of 2. Encourage them to think quietly about the questions before discussing with their partner. Creating a table or spreadsheet may help students organize the work in the second question. If needed, encourage students to do so.

Anticipated Misconceptions

Students may struggle to think of how to start finding the values for \(a\) and \(b\). Suggest that they make a table of corresponding values of \(t\) and \(y\), and think about starting with \(t\) and calculating \(y\).

They may also struggle with understanding a negative value for \(t\) referring to years. Emphasize that a value of \(t = -1\) represents the time 1 year before the estimate was made. Ask what a value of \(t = -4\) represents.

Activity Synthesis

Review how students found an equation, \(y=1,200\cdot 2^t\), to represent the volume of coral. Make sure that they recall that the 1,200 represents the 1,200 cubic centimeters of coral when it was first measured, and the 2 indicates the doubling of its volume each year.

Select students to share their interpretations of negative values of \(t\) and how they found the value of \(t\) when given a volume. Ask questions such as:

  • “In this situation, what does it mean to say that when \(t\) is –3, \(y\) is \(1,200\cdot 2^{-3}\) or 150?” (3 years before the coral was measured to be 1,200 cubic centimeters, its volume was 150 cubic centimeters.)
  • “How did you estimate or find \(t\) when the volume of the coral was 37.5 cubic centimeters?”

It may be helpful to display a graph of \(y=1,200\cdot 2^t\), such as the one embedded in the digital version of these materials. Display the graph (either with or without the points from the table plotted) and ask students to identify the volume of coral when \(t\) is –3 and the time when the volume of coral is 37.5 cubic centimeters.

If using the digital applet, consider initially turning off the function and the point, and hiding the expression list. Interpreting Negative Exponents in Exponential Growth


How to find the output value of an equation, \(y\), given \(d\), for any value of the domain

Step 1 - Substitute the value of the variable into the given equation.

Step 2 - Rewrite any negative exponent as a reciprocal. That is how many times the reciprocal of the base should be multiplied.

Step 3 - Multiply to find \(y\), the output value of the equation.


Let’s look at a problem involving an exponential growth equation and negative exponents.

A forest fire has been burning for several days. The burned area, in acres, is given by the equation \(y=4,800\cdot 2^d\), where \(d\) is the number of days since the area of the fire was first measured.

Review the first few entries of the table and then use them to help you complete examples 1 – 3.

\(d\), days since first measurement Process column \(y\), acres burned since fire started
0 \(= 4800 \cdot 2^0 = 4800 \cdot 1 \) 4800
–1 \(= 4800 \cdot 2^1 = 4800 \cdot \frac12 \) 2400
–2 a. _____ b. _____
–3 c. _____ d. _____
–5 e. _____ f. _____

Example 1

For parts “a” and “b” in the table, determine the value for \(y\) when \(d = -2\)

Step 1 - Substitute the given domain value into the expression.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-2}\)

Step 2 - Rewrite any negative exponent as a reciprocal.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-2}\) \(= 4800 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^2 = 4800 \cdot \frac14\)

Step 3 - Multiply to find \(y\).

\(y = 1200\)


Example 2

For parts “c” and “d” in the table, determine the value for\(y\) when \(d = -3\)

Step 1 - Substitute the given domain value into the expression.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-3}\)

Step 2 - Rewrite any negative exponent as a reciprocal.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-3}\) \(= 4800 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^3 = 4800 \cdot \frac18\)

Step 3 - Multiply to find \(y\).

\(y = 600\)


Example 3

For parts “e” and “f” in the table, determine the value for \(y\) when \(d = -5\)

Step 1 - Substitute the given domain value into the expression.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-5}\)

Step 2 - Rewrite any negative exponent as a reciprocal.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-5}\)\(= 4800 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})^5 = 4800 \cdot \frac{1}{32}\)

Step 3 - Multiply to find \(y\).

\(y = 150\)


Example 4

What does the value of \(y\) tell you about the area burned in the fire when \(d = -1\)?

Answer: The \(y\) value at \(d= -1\) represents the fact that 2,400 acres had burned 1 day before the first measurement was taken.


Example 5

How much area had the fire burned a week before it measured 4,800 acres? Explain your reasoning.

Answer: Since there are 7 days in a week, to find the area burned a week before the fire was measured, we need to determine the value of \(y\) when \(d = -7\).

Step 1 - Substitute the given domain value into the expression.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-7}\)

Step 2 - Rewrite any negative exponent as a reciprocal.

\(y = 4800 \cdot 2^{-7}\) \(= 4800 \cdot \frac12 ^7 = 4800 \cdot \frac{1}{128}\)

Step 3 - Multiply to find \(y\).

\(y = 37.5\)

\(37.5\) acres had burned a week before the fire measured \(4,800\) acres. The value is found by substituting \(-7\) for d since that represents \(7\) days before the first measurement of \(4,800\) acres.
